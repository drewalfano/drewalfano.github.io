01 · Branding & Packaging

Soo Falls

Reviving Sault Ste. Marie's historic brewery through a bold, flexible identity and packaging system built for modern retail, hospitality, events, and local culture.

Role

In-house Graphic Designer & Brand Lead

Timeline

December 2023 – March 2026; packaging design ongoing

Disciplines

Brand Identity, Packaging, Art Direction, Campaign Design, Social Media, Environmental Graphics, Outdoor Advertising, Print Collateral, Photography, Video, Website Strategy & Information Architecture, Web Design Direction, Production Design

The Challenge

Soo Falls needed a brand that felt distinctly local without leaning on tired waterfall clichés, and a packaging system flexible enough to scale across a growing product line.

The Approach

A bold wordmark, a restrained color system, and a modular label grid let each product feel distinct while staying unmistakably part of the same family.

Project Scale

Ten-plus produced can designs. LCBO launches. Bus wraps, billboards, and a Zamboni. One system built to hold all of it.

A System Built to Expand

The packaging system begins with five core beers, each using the same label structure while colour and typography distinguish the individual styles. From there, the system expands into the Shipwreck Sour series, a seasonal Hefeweizen and a limited Oktoberfest release.

The Doppelbock was intentionally designed to stand apart. Its darker palette, expressive typography and bold illustration give it an identity of its own, while the Soo Falls mark keeps it connected to the broader brand.

Logo Reconstruction and Modernization

The only logo material supplied at the beginning of the project was a collection of low-resolution raster references. I reconstructed the mark as a clean vector, refining its typography and proportions while retaining its established character.

Also on This Project

Website Strategy & Design Direction

Alongside the brand and packaging system, I planned the site's sitemap and page structure, selected the typography and colour direction, and chose and directed the photography used throughout — establishing the intended visual experience for the site. A developer built the production website from that direction.
