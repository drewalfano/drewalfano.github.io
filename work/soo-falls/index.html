01 — Brand Identity & Packaging

Soo Falls
Brewing Co.

Reviving Sault Ste. Marie's historic brewery with a bold, flexible identity — a packaging system built to scale from five core beers to a shelf full of seasonals, and to hold up everywhere from the LCBO to a wrapped Zamboni.

Visit the live site — soofallsbrewery.com ↗

Role

In-house Graphic Designer & Brand Lead

Timeline

Dec 2023 – Mar 2026 · packaging ongoing

Scope

Identity, packaging, campaigns, environmental, production

10+ Produced Can Designs Seasonal Packaging LCBO Launch Restaurant Partner Collateral Bus & Billboard Advertising Zamboni Wraps Event Branding Social Campaigns Signage & History Walls

The Challenge

Local pride, without the waterfall clichés.

Soo Falls needed a brand that read as distinctly Sault Ste. Marie without leaning on tired waterfall imagery, and that made craft beer feel approachable in a Northern Ontario town — never gimmicky, never intimidating, just a beer people want to try and aren't confused by. The packaging system also had to scale across a fast-growing product line without losing its family resemblance.

The Approach

One system, room for many beers.

A confident wordmark, a disciplined navy-anchored palette, and a modular label grid. Each beer gets its own colour and character; the shared structure keeps them unmistakably one family — on a shelf, a tap handle, or the side of a trailer.

02 — Packaging

Five core beers. One growing shelf.

The system started with the core lineup and stretched into the Shipwreck Sour series, a seasonal Hefeweizen, and standalone releases like the Doppelbock — each distinct, all clearly related.

03 — Logo Reconstruction

From low-res reference to clean vector.

The only mark supplied at kickoff was a set of low-resolution raster references. I rebuilt it as a clean vector — refining the typography, weight, and proportions while keeping the character that made it feel like it had been around since 1899.

Drag the handle to compare.

04 — On the Shelf

Built to hold up off the shelf, not just on it.

Retail was the real test. The Italian Pilsner carried the identity into LCBO stores across Sault Ste. Marie and the surrounding area; the Hazy IPA pushed it further still — onto LCBO shelves all the way to Sudbury, alongside the province's biggest brands.

Getting there meant designing inside two sets of technical requirements at once: LCBO and The Beer Store each spec their own label dimensions, mandatory copy, and layout rules before a can is even eligible for their shelves. The branded shelf boxes carried the same constraints — every panel had to hold up as functional retail packaging first, brand system second.

05 — Brand in the World

The identity had to work everywhere it showed up.

A wrapped Zamboni, a neon sign, tap handles, festival tents, merch, glass pours — the brand travelled well past the can. I built and ran Soo Falls' social presence from day one, and directed the look everywhere it landed.

06 — Role & Rollout

Design that followed the work to the finish line.

Beyond the marks and labels, I planned the website's sitemap, pages, typography, colour, and imagery direction — a developer built the final site. The same hands-on approach carried into production: proofing labels, coordinating the canning line, and solving problems as work moved from file to finished can.

Design and development were credited separately. My part was the identity system and every place it showed up — which, for a brewery in a proud town, turned out to be just about everywhere.
