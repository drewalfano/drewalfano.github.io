02 · Brand Systems & Campaign Design

Canal District

One district, multiple distinct experiences — ongoing brand, campaign, and production work across a connected group of hospitality and entertainment venues in Sault Ste. Marie.

Role

In-house Graphic Designer & Brand Lead

Timeline

May 2024 – March 2026

Disciplines

Brand Systems, Campaign Design, Event & Venue Marketing, Production Design, Motion Design

Selected Applications

Campaign Design Menu Design Merchandise Event Collateral Social Media Motion Design

The Challenge

Canal District brings together a pub, a wood-oven pizza restaurant, a steakhouse, a concert and event venue, a dessert shop, an outfitter, and a seasonal outdoor rink under one shared destination. Each venue already had its own personality — the work was keeping campaigns, seasonal promotions, and physical applications consistent across all of them without flattening what made each one distinct.

The Approach

Ongoing production across every venue's existing identity — seasonal menus, event campaigns, merchandise, signage, and social content — plus original campaign work built for the district as a whole, like the concert series brought to The Machine Shop.

One District, Multiple Distinct Experiences

Seven venues, one shared destination — each one recognizable on its own, and consistent as part of the whole.

A System Built to Repeat

The same staff-shirt template, carried across venues — each one keeping its own mark and voice inside a consistent production system.

Campaign Design

The Machine Shop's concert series brought touring acts to Sault Ste. Marie — campaign work built for a single show, exported across every size a real promotional rollout needs, from a social post to an 11×17 print.

Identity & Motion — Whiskey River

A logo and a produced television advertisement built from the ground up for Whiskey River — a rare case within this project where the identity itself, not just its application, is original work.

Logo design & motion design — asset pending final export

Extending the Brands into Daily Communication

Beyond individual campaigns and physical applications, the role included managing the venues' ongoing social presence — each account recognizable as part of the broader district while speaking in a tone appropriate to its own audience, events, and offers.

Soo Falls Brewing Co. was the clearest example: its social presence (@soofallsbrewingco) was built organically from the ground up — visual language, content rhythm, and campaign assets developed alongside the brewery's own launch and growth.
