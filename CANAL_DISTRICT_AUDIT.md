## 10. Proposed case-study narratives

### Direction A — Multi-brand system

**Core idea:** One designer maintaining a consistent visual language and
production quality across seven-plus distinct venue identities inside a shared
destination.

**Recommended section order:** Hero (wayfinding photo or logo composite) → The
challenge (maintaining consistency across many brands under one roof) → Venue
identity system (logo suite) → Menu system → Campaign/event work (Buckle Up
Tour) → Merch system → Real-world photography → Reflection/closing.

**Supporting assets:** logo suite, shirt system, menu system, fridge-wrap
redesign (if confirmed), rink boards.

**Advantages:** matches the strongest, most repeatable evidence (logo suites +
merch system are the most "systemized" proof); plays to Drew's existing
About-page framing ("systems... coherent across... applications").

**Risks:** requires manual export of many `.ai` logo files before it can be built;
depends on confirming authorship of each identity (§4, Q1).

**Facts still required:** which identities were designed vs. inherited (Q1–Q2).

### Direction B — Destination marketing

**Core idea:** Canal District as a connected hospitality/entertainment
destination — the story is the *place* (a historic train-station complex now
home to a brewery, restaurants, an event venue, dessert shop, and outdoor rink),
told through its marketing.

**Recommended section order:** Hero (wayfinding photo) → The challenge (make a
multi-venue district feel like one place worth visiting) → Around the district
(venue photography grid) → Seasonal campaign calendar (Easter/Mother's
Day/summer) → Signature event (Buckle Up Tour concert) → Closing wide shot.
